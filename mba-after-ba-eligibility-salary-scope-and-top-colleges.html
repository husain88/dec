MBA After BA: Eligibility, Salary, Scope, and Top Colleges

Bachelor of Arts (BA) is the most common undergraduate degree course; most students complete it after they complete schooling in different fields. It is confirmed that the BA course is chosen by students who want to enter the field of government exam preparation. However, they can find jobs in various private and public sectors too, such as mass communication and journalism, social work, environmental planning, and others. To advance their career and enhance their knowledge, some students might choose MBA courses in different specializations after graduation and open doors for new opportunities.

MBA is a viable option for BA graduates to enter the world of business administration, corporations, and working with budding entrepreneurs. An MBA after a BA from a top university can break the mold, allowing students to enter directly into highly competitive business sectors with high salary packages and other benefits included.

This blog is to guide students to learn more about MBA courses, eligibility, scope, salary, and the top colleges to pursue an MBA after a BA. Give it a run!

Why Choose an MBA After a BA?

After a BA degree, choosing an MBA could be an option for candidates due to the advantages it holds:

  • After the completion of a BA, if students choose to pursue an MBA, they can have a better opportunity to grow professionally in the field of business and management. Students can enhance their professional and business skills by pursuing an MBA. They have advanced options to grow and build their careers in the right direction.
  • Students choosing an MBA could learn the art of adopting a dynamic nature and making themselves ready for the corporate world. They will not miss opportunities coming their way from lucrative companies; to work in these companies, they need administrative and technical skills that can be enhanced by obtaining an MBA degree.
  • Students can acquire skills to be aware of the competition, and they can also fulfill the recruiting standards of recruiters.
  • An MBA program allows students to develop their personal qualities and gain better exposure to the business world, including through projects, placements, internships, group discussions, and other required skills to be rigid in this world.
  • BA courses are known for their theoretical knowledge, whereas MBA courses are known for their practicality and growing skills, which are significantly different from each other.
  • An MBA after a BA helps students boost their knowledge and skills and enhance their growth in a very competitive manner, making them ready for financial and economic challenges.

MBA After BA: Eligibility, Scope, and Salary

Around the globe, several universities provide MBA courses after graduation to students of any stream. There is an eligibility criteria for these courses; different specializations have different scopes with worthy salary packages. Let’s take a look at each of them one by one.

Eligibility Critеria

  • Students with a minimum score of 45–55% in the BA graduation can apply for the MBA entrance exams such as the CAT, GMAT, XAT, MAT, and other common exams.
  • Some universities have additional entrance exams other than the national entrance exams for MBA admissions; you have to clear both of these exams to get admission to the relevant MBA courses at these universities.
  • Before final admission, students are invited to the final interview process, which is mandatory for selected candidates. To fill out the mandatory candidates, some universities practice organizing group discussions during this phase.
  • Most universities require 2-3 years of working experience before applying for MBA courses; however, this requirement can vary from university to university.
  • Candidates must have cleared the minimum required score in the GMAT and GRE entrance exams before they will be eligible for the admission test at the university.

Coursеs and Their Scope

  • MBA courses after BA include specialization in particular areas, such as MBA in marketing and finance, MBA in human resources, MBA in business analytics, MBA in sales and management, and many others.
  • The scope for the graduates of the BA is in the field of startups after they are done with the MBA courses and specialization in the same. The MBA candidates are guided in the direction that they can better understand the algorithms of business companies and how they are organized and managed.
  • MBA graduates after this are not only known to operate their businesses or start-ups, but they are also known to work in managerial roles that include marketing, logistics, human resources, and others. Digital marketing, hotel management, and economics management are considered when MBA graduates are hired following their BA degree.

Salary Packagеs

  • In the end, it all comes down to the salary packages and the money spent on the studies as an investment. The initial move towards earning money after graduation and postgraduation comes with a lot of challenges, and students have several options, including corporations, MNCs, IT sectors, and other related fields
  • After earning an MBA degree, students can apply for job opportunities that are related to their specialization courses and earn handsome salary packages to secure their personal lives. There is huge scope for salary advancement with the increase in candidate experience.
  • Along with the jobs in these reputable companies, one can learn integrated skills that can help them build their own business with a pronouncеd startup feel.
